VERBAL BULLYING


Verbal bullying involves bullying others with degrading cruel demeaning language.



Verbal bullying includes:
  • verbal abuse
  • racism
  • sexist or homophobic comments
  • insulting jokes
  • sexually suggestive comments
  • offensive language of any kind
Ways to tell if your child is being verbally bullied:
  • They may become moody, withdrawn, or their appetites may change.
  • They may speak up about what someone said that hurt their feelings, or ask if what someone said about them was true.

    Countering verbal bullying can be difficult.

    The best way to appropiately deal with verbal bullying involves building your child's confidence and making it easier for them to confide in You.

    We are all prone to bullying, how we choose to overcome it depends on the type of support system we have,the emotional strength we rely on and mostly the courage we have to step out of it.

    Confident children are less likely to be verbally bullied than children who are already suffering from low self-esteem and self-image,hence self-motivation and self-encouragement plays a big role in building one's emotional strength

    Students should be taught in the classroom to treat everyone with respect and never be mean or rude to others.
