SOCIAL BULLYING

What is Social Bullying

Social bullying is the use of various means to damage or destroy someone’s social relationships. For this reason, it’s often called relational aggression or relational bullying.

It’s an insidious form of bullying because it takes many forms.

Social bullying can occur in person and as a form of cyberbullying.

Social bullying is intentional, repetitive, and aggressive social behavior aimed at harming others.

This type of behavior typically includes verbal abuse, gossip, or other behavior that causes psychological and emotional harm to the victim and causes social isolation.

Places where this type of bullying occurs include schools, sporting events, colleges, homes and businesses, and neighborhoods.

The purpose of social bullying is to belittle and harm another person or group. For example, in middle school, bullying is sometimes practiced by making fun of unpopular children. Making fun of other children about how they dress, making fun of how they speak, making fun of their academic ability, race or culture are behaviors that bullies may do to gain power over other children in order to get their children.

This kind of bullying is hard to spot and often happens behind the scenes of the bully. including:

  • lie, spread false rumors, spread gossip
  • encourage others to turn against someone
  • Constantly ignoring someone and encouraging others to do the same
  • Socially excluding someone or making a negative comment on a post or photo on the Internet
  • Acts that damage a person's social reputation or acceptance
  • Use demeaning nicknames and move on when asked to stop

It is not easy for those going through this situation to accept that they have crossed the line between pranks and jokes and ongoing bullying. Seeking help can be more difficult if you find yourself being bullied
